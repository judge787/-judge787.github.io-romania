Fun Facts about Romania


  • Romania is a remarkable place, full with interesting stories, unique sites, breathtaking scenery, and fascinating people. The country is rich in natural, architectural, and artistic marvels, from Europe's best-preserved delta to the world's largest outdoor museum.
  • Romania's media market is one of the most active in Southeastern Europe. The majority of Romanians like to watch television. The state-owned TVR and commercial stations Pro TV and Antena 1 have the most viewers, however there are many smaller, private channels, some of which are part of local networks.
  • Romania is in Europe and borders the black sea​. Bordering countries include:​ Hungary​, Ukraine​, Moldova, Bulgaria​, Yugoslavia​
  • Romanian cuisines are a diverse mix of dishes from various traditions with which it has come into contact, but it retains its own identity. It was largely influenced by Turkish, Greece, and Bulgarian backgrounds. Their traditional cuisine is known as the “Sarmale” which is a roll consisting of minced meat or other stuffing wrapped in cabbage leaves.
  • The tricolour design of Romania's flag includes three colours. The colour blue can be found on the hoist side. The colour blue is supposed to symbolise liberty. The central strip is yellow and represents justice. The last band is red, which depicts the nation's fraternity.
