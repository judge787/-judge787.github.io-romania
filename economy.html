Economy

Romania's Economy

Romania's economy is ranked 35th in the world, with an annual output of $585 billion dollars (PPP). ​Romania is Central and Eastern Europe's largest electronics manufacturer. ​Romania has experienced some of the strongest growth rates in the EU in recent years, with rates of 4.8 percent in 2016, 7.1 percent in 2017, 4.4 percent in 2018, and 4.1 percent in 2019. ​ GDP Romania's economy is a fast-growing, high-income mixed economy with a high Human Development Index and a skilled labour force, ranking 12th in total nominal GDP and 7th when adjusted for purchasing power parity in the European Union.

Romania's Top Imports and Exports

Vehicle Parts ($6.89 billion), Cars ($5.53 billion), Insulated Wire ($4.09 billion), Refined Petroleum ($2.37 billion), and Electrical Control Boards ($2.32 billion) are Romania's top exports, with the majority going to Germany ($17.3 billion), Italy ($8.17 billion), France ($5.17 billion), Hungary ($3.32 billion), and the United Kingdom ($2.83 billion). Romania was the world's largest exporter of sunflower seeds in 2019 ($815 million).

Vehicle Parts ($4.2 billion), Crude Petroleum ($3.73 billion), Cars ($3.23 billion), Packaged Medicaments ($2.93 billion), and Insulated Wire ($1.96 billion) are Romania's top imports, with the majority coming from Germany ($18.4 billion), Italy ($8.75 billion), Hungary ($6.21 billion), Poland ($5.66 billion), and China ($4.92 billion).
