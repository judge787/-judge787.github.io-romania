Government and Politics

Romania's Politics and Government

Romania is a democratic parliamentary republic. The Senate (upper house), which has 136 representatives, and the Chamber of Deputies (lower house), which has 329 seats, make up the bicameral parliamentary system. Both are elected directly for four years using proportional representation and party lists. The Prime Minister is the Head of Government in Romania's semi-presidential political system, whereas the President is the Head of State. The President of Romania is directly elected for a five-year term with the option of re-election once. The President appoints the Prime Minister, with Parliament's consent, and is also the commander-in-chief of the armed forces. Although the President is unable to originate legislation, he can return measures to the House of Representatives for additional consideration. The Republic was created on November 21, 1991, under a Constitution agreed by Parliament and confirmed by a national vote on December 8, 1991 which is their of goverment. The two-chamber Parliament (the Chamber of Deputies and the Senate), which is elected for a four-year term, is the people's main representative body and only law-making authority.​ A popular vote is used to elect the president. The government is responsible for overseeing the public sector, with Parliament's permission.​ Klaus Lohannis is their current president​. The Parliament Palace in Romania’s capital of Bucharest is the second-largest building in the world, behind only the Pentagon. The building is 84m high. It is also the heaviest building in the world.
